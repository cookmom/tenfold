# Islamic Geometric Patterns — Research

Research repository exploring the mathematics, history, and computational methods behind Islamic geometric art.

## Scope

**Historic**: From the earliest Islamic geometric motifs (9th century) through the golden age of zellige, girih, and muqarnas to contemporary practitioners.

**Computational**: Algorithms for generating, analyzing, and understanding Islamic patterns — from Hankin's "polygons in contact" to quasicrystal tilings.

**State of the Art**: Modern tools, research papers, and artists pushing the boundaries of Islamic geometric design.

## Structure

```
research/          — Notes, analyses, paper summaries
algorithms/        — Implementations of pattern generation methods
references/        — Key papers, books, artist profiles
gallery/           — Generated patterns + source images for study
```

## Philosophy

> "Islamic geometric patterns are one of the major forms of Islamic ornament... 
> constructed on grids that require only ruler and compass to draw."
> — Wikipedia

Two tools. Infinite patterns. We study why.
