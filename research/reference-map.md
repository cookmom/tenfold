## Essential Books

### Pattern
- **Islamic Patterns** — Keith Critchlow (Thames & Hudson) — seminal text, symbolism of forms
- **Islamic Design: A Genius for Geometry** — Daud Sutton (Wooden Books) — "best thing published in recent years"
- **Geometric Concepts in Islamic Art** — Issam El-Said & Ayse Parman — patterns in architectural context
- **Zillij: Art of Moroccan Ceramics** — Damlugi (Garnet) — photographic study + pattern analysis
- **Arabic Geometrical Pattern and Designs** — J. Bourgoin (Dover) — design source with under-drawings
- **The Sense of Unity** — Nadar Ardalan & Laleh Bakhtiar — symbolism of geometry and architecture
- **Profound Patterns** — Adam Williamson — artwork from 65 online classes, 20K registrations
- **Arts and Crafts of the Islamic Lands** — Various (incl. Adam Williamson) — techniques and methods

### Islimi/Arabesque
- **Motifs in Turkish Decorative Art** — Birol & Derman — Turkish/Iranian floral motifs
- **Iranian Garden, Golden Twist** — M.R Honarvar — compositional layouts
- **Splendours of Qur'an Calligraphy and Illumination** — Martin Lings — illuminated manuscripts

### Cultural/Spiritual
- **Art of Islam** — Titus Burckhardt — images + symbolism
- **Islamic Art and Spirituality** — Seyyed Hossein Nasr — accessible, respected scholar
- **Sacred Art in East and West** — Titus Burckhardt — intro to Islamic art

## The Three Disciplines of Islamic Art
1. **Geometry** (handasah) — foundational, lower walls/floors
2. **Islimi/Arabesque** — biomorphic/floral, middle zone
3. **Calligraphy** (khatt) — highest, domes/upper walls
